The Evolution of Home Healthcare

Historical Context

1800s: Physicians making house calls as primary care model

1960s: Medicare & Medicaid establish formal home health services

1990s: Digital records begin transforming care coordination

2020s: AI & remote monitoring create new home care possibilities

Current Challenges

Staffing Shortages

79.2% turnover rate in home healthcare workforce

Projected shortage of 400,000 home health aides by 2025

Aging Population

1.5 billion adults 65+ globally by 2025

22% of US population will be 65+ by 2040

Rising Healthcare Costs

Healthcare costs approaching 20% of GDP

Unsustainable growth trajectory without intervention

Administrative Burden

Caregivers spend up to 25% of time on documentation

70% of nurses report burnout from excessive paperwork
